# All-Star & Tournament Teams — Region 58 (Postseason)

> Postseason program. Governed by Region 58 Policy & Guidelines. Invitation-only.

## Eligibility (Policy & Guidelines, Section II.B)
A player is eligible for an All-Star / Tournament team if they meet **only three** requirements:
1. **Registered with Region 58** for that Membership Year's **Fall season**.
2. **Played at least ½ of the team's league season games** (playing ≥ ¾ of a game counts as having played that game; RC may grant exceptions for documented injury/illness).
3. **Signed a Commitment Letter** (player and parents) if requested.
- There is **no** published requirement to obtain a signed form/permission from an outside (club) coach. Adding requirements mid-season is not supported by published policy. New requirements must be added formally **before** the season starts. [VERIFY]

## Invitation-Only Registration (PlayMetrics)
- Postseason (All-Star and Tournament) registration is **by invitation only**, ONLY for players who **participated in the Fall program AND were invited by a coach**.
- **There is NO fee** for postseason registration.
- **GOTCHA:** If a **$25 "AYSO Membership Fee"** appears during postseason registration, the family must **STOP** and not proceed — it means the system isn't recognizing the player as a Fall participant. Direct them to contact **registrar@ayso58.org**. [VERIFY]
- An **"I Accept" confirmation gate** has families confirm both requirements (Fall participation + invitation). Non-invited families should exit.

## Declining All-Star → Tournament Eligibility (Section IV.B)
- **Default:** a player who **declines an All-Star team is NOT eligible** for a Tournament team.
- **Exceptions** (player REMAINS eligible for a Tournament team):
  - Declining to play with a sibling on the other team, or because a parent is HC/AC of the other team.
  - **(a)** Cannot make the All-Star time commitment.
  - **(b)** Another reason deemed adequate by the RC.
- **Reading note:** A player who declines All-Star **specifically because of the time commitment IS eligible** for a Tournament team under exception (a) — do not read this in reverse.
- A player who declined/left a team may not participate in any tournament where their former team is also competing.

## Commissioner (RC) Authority
- **Section V:** RC may act in the Region's best interests in case of omission/ambiguity/error — a gap-filler, not an override of explicit provisions.
- **Section II.F:** RC may (sole, reasonable discretion) declare a player ineligible (e.g., prior misconduct) or declare an otherwise-ineligible player eligible (e.g., All-Star-caliber player injured mid-season).
- **Section III:** Tournament-team selection largely left to the Tournament team Head Coach with DM oversight; strong consideration for highly-rated non-All-Star players and those showing commitment; RC guides when necessary.
- The RC is the sole Executive Member of the Region.
- **Fairness note:** Selectively applying an eligibility requirement to only one division (e.g., U12 Girls but not others) raises fairness/discrimination concerns under AYSO's Code of Conduct.

## Commitment Letters
- Both All-Star and Tournament teams require a signed **Player & Parent Commitment Letter**; affirmative response due **within 48 hours** of receipt.
- **All-Star letter:** make AYSO the first priority through end of season AND mandatory participation in all **Area Play and Western States** tournaments (regardless of advancement).
- **Tournament letter:** materially different — **OMITS** the mandatory Area/Section/Western States language (important for club players weighing conflicts).
- Both require: **75% practice attendance, 90% tournament-game attendance** (advance notice of absences); on-time payments; referee volunteering if certified (else other volunteering); good sportsmanship; compliance with coach's game decisions and RC rulings.
- Non-compliance consequences: reduced playing time, removal from roster, suspension, loss of future postseason eligibility, or other consequences approved by the Commissioner.

## Tournament Season Logistics
- Runs **mid-December through June**; tournaments hosted by AYSO regions across Southern California (mostly local, a few with optional overnight stays).
- Played Saturdays/Sundays, except one three-day tournament over **Presidents' Day weekend**.
- Teams typically enter **5–10 tournaments**; coach and parents decide how many.
- **Official All-Star Tournament stages:** Area Play (late January) → Section Play in Bakersfield (mid-February, if qualified) → Western States (late March/early April, if qualified).
- **Costs:** tournament registration fee + referee deposit averaging **~$50–70 per player per tournament**, paid to Region 58. Referee deposits refunded if all referee assignments are covered (refunds typically not processed until end of calendar year).
- **Uniforms ~$50–70**; Turkey Tournament participants reuse that uniform for postseason. Optional items (backpacks, sweatshirts) may be available.
